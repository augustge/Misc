A measure of the resultant speed of an oscillator when a driving force is applied to it.
Consider the standard harmonic oscillator equation $\ddot{x}+\omega_0^2 x = 0$. First, let's make this a bit more reasonable by adding some damping term: $\ddot{x}+\gamma \dot{x}+\omega_0^2 x = 0$. If we apply an harmonic force $Ae^{i\omega t}$, then we are left with the following inhomogeneous equation \[ \ddot{x}+\gamma \dot{x}+\omega_0 x = Ae^{i\omega t}. \] Since we are forcing a frequency $\omega$ on the system, let's guess the solution is of the form \[ x = Ce^{i\omega t}. \] This makes sense if \[ C = \frac{A}{i\gamma \omega+\omega_0^2-\omega^2}. \] In particular, this means that the oscillator will have a speed \[ v = \dot{x} = \frac{i\omega}{i\gamma \omega+\omega_0^2-\omega^2} A e^{i\omega t} \] we shall refer to the quantity dividing the applied force \[ Z = \gamma + i\frac{\omega^2-\omega_0^2}{\omega} \] as the \textit{accoustic impedance} of the system. More generally, the acoustic impedance is defined by \[ Z \equiv \frac{\text{driving force}}{\text{resultant speed}} \] where both the driving force and the speed are treated as complex.
